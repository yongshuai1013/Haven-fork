• A connection through a jump host that goes quiet now fails instead of spinning forever. Because a jump connection runs over a tunnelling channel rather than a socket, the connect timeout never applied to it: a far side that accepted the channel but never replied left Haven waiting indefinitely, with no error and nothing in the connection log. It now gives the far side a deadline to say hello and reports which hop went quiet. Typing a password, a TOTP code or touching a security key is never rushed by it.
